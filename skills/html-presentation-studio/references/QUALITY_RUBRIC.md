# Quality rubric

A very good deck should score at least 8/10 in every applicable dimension and have no HIGH issue.

## Dimensions

### 1. Communication objective

- 10: audience, action, and takeaway are unmistakable.
- 7: clear but inconsistently reinforced.
- 4: informative without a decision or remembered conclusion.
- 1: unclear purpose.

### 2. Narrative coherence

- 10: each slide creates the need for the next; titles form the abstract.
- 7: sensible order with minor redundancy.
- 4: topical grouping without an arc.
- 1: disconnected slides.

### 3. Source fidelity

- 10: every material claim/asset is traceable; uncertainty is honest.
- 7: sources exist, but linkage is incomplete.
- 4: several unverifiable numbers/attributions.
- 1: fabricated or misleading evidence.

Wrong numbers, invented quotes, fake customers, or deceptive charts are HIGH.

### 4. Title quality

- 10: assertion titles express conclusions and progress the story.
- 7: mix of assertions and labels.
- 4: mostly labels.
- 1: titles absent or misleading.

### 5. Readability

- 10: content is grasped in seconds; type is comfortable; language direct.
- 7: occasional dense slide.
- 4: repeated walls, jargon, or small type.
- 1: deck must be read rather than presented.

### 6. Hierarchy and art direction

- 10: distinctive, content-appropriate system and deliberate emphasis.
- 7: consistent and professional but conventional.
- 4: template-like or inconsistent.
- 1: hierarchy obscures meaning.

### 7. Information design

- 10: correct visual form, direct annotation, low clutter.
- 7: mostly correct with minor redundancy.
- 4: prose where visuals are needed or decorative visuals.
- 1: misleading/incomprehensible.

### 8. Technical quality

- 10: no overflow, broken assets, console errors, navigation or export defects.
- 7: one minor responsive issue.
- 4: multiple visible defects.
- 1: unreliable to present.

Clipped essential content or broken navigation is HIGH.

### 9. Accessibility

- 10: semantics, keyboard, focus, reduced motion, alt text, non-color cues.
- 7: usable with one secondary gap.
- 4: keyboard/contrast barriers.
- 1: inaccessible.

### 10. Presenter readiness

- 10: timing, notes, transitions, fallback, sources, closing action ready.
- 7: presentable with minor rehearsal fixes.
- 4: content exists but delivery mechanics unfinished.
- 1: not usable in the setting.

## Severity

### HIGH

- false/unverifiable material claim;
- essential content clipped/unreadable;
- broken navigation or core asset;
- severe contrast/accessibility barrier;
- secret or unsafe script;
- title contradicts evidence.

### MEDIUM

- dense slide;
- topic-label title;
- weak responsive layout;
- missing source footnote;
- inconsistent design token;
- unnecessary animation.

### LOW

- minor alignment, rhythm, wording, or polish.

## Verdict

- **EXCELLENT:** average ≥9, every dimension ≥8, no HIGH, browser pass complete.
- **STRONG:** average ≥8, no dimension below 7, no HIGH.
- **ACCEPTABLE:** average ≥7, no HIGH, limited MEDIUM issues.
- **REVISION NEEDED:** any HIGH or any dimension below 6.
